Javin Hunter is a former wide receiver in the National Football League. He was picked in the sixth round of the 2002 NFL Draft by the Baltimore Ravens after playing collegiate football at Notre Dame. He also played for the San Francisco 49ers for a season. Hunter’s father, James, was also a pro football player, and his son, Jaden Ivey, is a Purdue basketball player.

Hunter went to Detroit Country Day School in Beverly Hills, Michigan, where he was a three-time All-State and USA Today All-American basketball player and won three consecutive state titles alongside former Miami Heat player and Duke star Shane Battier.

In football, he was a Parade All-American, a USA Today second-team All-American, The Sporting News’ 34th greatest player, and the Chicago Sun-Times’ 37th best player. The Dallas Morning News, PrepStar, and Street and Smith magazines all rated him as one of the top three wide receivers in the country. Hunter was a top recruit for Urban Meyer, the famed and former Notre Dame wide receiver coach, and he committed to Notre Dame.
