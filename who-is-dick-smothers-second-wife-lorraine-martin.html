Who is Dick Smothers second wife, Lorraine Martin?

September 2024 · 2 minute read

Dick Smothers, born on November 20, 1938, in the heart of New York City, has not only left an indelible mark in the world of entertainment but has also crafted a complex personal narrative. In this spotlight, we delve into the intriguing story of Dick Smothers’ second wife, Lorraine Martin, and the shared chapters of their lives.

Early Life and Career

Standing tall at 5’9″ (1.75 m), Dick Smothers, fondly known as Dickie, has been a versatile actor and writer, recognized for his roles in notable productions such as “Casino” (1995) and “The Smothers Brothers Comedy Hour” (1967).

Marital Journey

Lorraine Wallace (September 19, 1986 – May 30, 1994)

Lorraine Wallace holds a distinctive place in the chronicles of Dick Smothers’ love life. They entered into matrimony on September 19, 1986, and their journey lasted until May 30, 1994. The union brought forth two children, creating a family dynamic that added both joy and complexity to Smothers’ life.

Family Ties

The Smothers family history is intertwined with military service, resilience, and tragedy. Major Thomas Bolyn Smothers Jr., Dick’s father, was a West Point graduate and a POW in World War II. Initially believed to have perished during the infamous Bataan Death March, he survived only to pass away later on a Japanese POW ship, laid to rest at sea. Dick’s mother, Ruth Genevieve Remick, played a pivotal role in navigating the challenges of raising Dick and his brother Tom.

Trivia and Recognition

  • Dick Smothers and his brother Tom were awarded a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame for Television at 6555 Hollywood Boulevard in Hollywood, California.
  • Despite both appearing in “The Informant!” (2009), the characters played by Dick and Tom Smothers were unrelated and never shared the screen.
  • Dick Smothers is the younger brother of Tom Smothers and a distant cousin of Alexandria Smothers.
  • He attended San Jose State University (SJSU), known then as San Jose State College.
  • Dick graduated from Redondo Union High School in Redondo Beach, California, in 1957.
  • He is the brother-in-law of Marcy Carriker.
