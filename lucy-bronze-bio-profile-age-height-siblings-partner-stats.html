Lucy Bronze Bio: Profile, Age, Height, Siblings, Partner, Stats

Lucy Bronze is an English professional footballer who plays as a right-back for Liga f club Barcelona and the women national team of England.  Her style of play has been compared to the German player, Philipp Lahm. She is a versatile right-back who is often deployed as an inverted full-back. She is also a creative player with speed and stamina.

Early Life

Lucy Bronze was born Lucia Roberta Tough Bronze on October 28, 1991 in  Berwick-upon-Tweed, England. She was born to parents Joaquim Bronze and Diana née Tough. Her father is Portuguese while her mother is from England. She has two siblings, an older brother named Jorge and a younger sister named Sophie.

She went to Duchess’s Community High School. She studied at the University of North Carolina. She transfered to Leeds Metropolitan University to continue with her sports science degree and graduated in 2013. Prior to being a footballer, she had wanted to become an accountant.

Bronze can also speak languages like English, Portuguese and French. She began to learn Spanish when she wanted to join Barcelona.

Career

Bronze began her football career at the youth level with Sunderland in 2002 and moved to Blyth Town in 2004.

She made her professional career with Sunderland in 2007 before moving to Everton in 2010.

She joined Liverpool in 2012 and later signed for Manchester City in November 2014.

She signed a three-year contract for Lyon in August 2017 and later returned to Manchester City on a two-year contract on September 8, 2020.

Bronze joined Barcelona in June 2022, after her contract with Manchester City expired. She scored her first goal for the club in a match against Athletico Madrid on November 27, 2022.

At the international stage, Bronze represented England at the Under-17, Under-19, under-20, and under-23 levels before being called up to the senior team. She made her debut at the Engalnd senior team on June 26, 2013, where she came on as a substitute for Dunia Susi at the 67th minute in a friendly match against Japan. She scored her first goal for England in a 3-0 victory over Belarus on June 14, 2014. She played for England at the 2023 FIFA Women’s World Cup which is her third consecutive appearance at the World Cup.
